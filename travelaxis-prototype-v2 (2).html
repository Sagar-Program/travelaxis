Destinations

Asia

The world's most diverse continent — from Himalayan passes to tropical islands. Below are the countries worth visiting and our honest guides for each.

🇮🇳 India

Snow-capped mountains in the north, dense rainforests in the east, desert forts in the west and temple towns in the south. No country covers more ground or demands more time.

Ladakh Guide → Meghalaya Guide → Rajasthan Guide →
🇮🇩 Indonesia

17,000 islands stretching across three time zones. Bali is just the start — Lombok, Flores and the Gili Islands all offer the same landscapes with fewer crowds and lower prices.

Bali Guide →
🇹🇭 Thailand

Still the easiest first trip abroad for most travelers — a genuinely forgiving country with great street food, affordable stays and enough variety to fill a month without repeating yourself.

Thailand Guide →
🇵🇭 Philippines

7,600 islands and some of the cheapest island-hopping in Southeast Asia. The challenge isn't finding a beautiful beach — it's choosing which lagoon to swim in first.

Palawan Guide →
🇰🇷 South Korea

Ultra-efficient cities with centuries-old palaces sitting between glass skyscrapers. The food culture alone — street tteokbokki, late-night pojangmacha, ramen at 2am — justifies the trip.

Seoul Guide →
🇻🇳 Vietnam

Limestone karsts rising from Halong Bay, lantern-lit streets in Hoi An, and $2 pho bowls at every corner. One of the best-value countries in Southeast Asia for food, culture and scenery.

🇯🇵 Japan

Ancient temples and neon convenience stores within walking distance of each other. Japan is more affordable than its reputation suggests — if you eat where locals eat and travel by JR Pass.

🇳🇵 Nepal

Home to 8 of the world's 10 highest peaks and some of the most rewarding trekking on earth. Even without a summit attempt, the Annapurna Circuit is a life-changing walk.

Destinations

Europe

Ancient cities, island coastlines and slow train journeys through wine country. Europe is large enough that you could spend a lifetime exploring and never repeat yourself.

🇬🇷 Greece

Whitewashed villages perched above caldera cliffs, ancient ruins with no queue before 8am, and olive oil so good it changes your opinion of salad. Visit in May or September — same views, half the price.

Santorini Guide →
🇮🇹 Italy

Renaissance frescoes, coastal villages clinging to cliffs and a regional food culture where every 50 miles changes the menu entirely. Rome for a week is not enough. Neither is a month.

Guide coming soon
🇵🇹 Portugal

Western Europe's best-value destination. Lisbon's tram-lined hills, Porto's riverside wine cellars and the Algarve's sea-stack beaches — all for prices that feel more like Southeast Asia than central Europe.

Guide coming soon
🇪🇸 Spain

Late dinners at 10pm, pintxos bars in the Basque Country, Gaudí's surreal architecture in Barcelona and flamenco in Seville. Spain has the most diverse landscapes of any European country and rarely disappoints.

Guide coming soon
🇫🇷 France

Paris is chapter one. The real France is lavender fields in Provence, oyster shacks on the Atlantic coast and medieval villages in the Dordogne that have barely changed in 500 years.

Guide coming soon
🇭🇷 Croatia

A thousand kilometres of Adriatic coastline, walled medieval cities and islands you'll want to return to every summer. Dubrovnik gets the headlines but Split and the Dalmatian islands are the real reason to come.

Destinations

Africa

Desert crossings, mountain summits and wildlife encounters that no documentary fully prepares you for.

🇲🇦 Morocco

Sahara dunes you reach by camel, ancient medinas you need three days to stop getting lost in, and Atlas mountain villages where the pace of life hasn't changed in centuries.

Sahara Guide →
🇹🇿 Tanzania

Kilimanjaro's glaciated summit, the Serengeti's wildebeest migration and Zanzibar's spice-scented coastline — often combined into one trip by travelers who can't choose between them.

Kilimanjaro Guide →
🇰🇪 Kenya

The Masai Mara at dawn, flamingos on Lake Nakuru and Nairobi's surprisingly vibrant food scene. Kenya is the classic East Africa safari base and still the benchmark everything else is measured against.

Destinations

Americas

Patagonian glaciers in the south, Mexican food markets in the north and everything in between — the Americas reward travelers who go beyond the obvious stops.

🇲🇽 Mexico

Oaxacan mole and mezcal, Yucatán cenotes, Mexico City's world-class museums and colonial towns that most visitors fly straight over. Mexico is far more than its beach resorts.

Oaxaca Guide →
🇦🇷 Argentina / 🇨🇱 Chile

Patagonia straddles both countries and offers some of the most dramatic hiking scenery anywhere on earth. Torres del Paine in Chile and El Chaltén in Argentina are a natural pair — most trekkers do both.

Patagonia Guide →
🇵🇪 Peru

Machu Picchu is on everyone's list for good reason, but the Sacred Valley, Huacachina desert oasis and Lima's world-ranked restaurant scene make Peru worth a month, not a week.

Destinations

Middle East

Ancient trade routes, Nabataean ruins and desert camps under a sky that still feels genuinely dark at night. The Middle East is far more welcoming to travelers than most people assume.

🇯🇴 Jordan

Petra is one of the few places that actually lives up to its photographs. Pair it with a night in Wadi Rum's silence and the Dead Sea's surreal buoyancy and you have the most complete week in the region.

Jordan Guide →
🇦🇪 UAE — Dubai

More affordable than its reputation suggests if you know where to stay and eat. Old Dubai's labyrinthine souks and the abra ride across the creek are free. The Burj Khalifa at sunrise costs $30.

Dubai Guide →
🇴🇲 Oman

Fjord-like coastlines in the north, vast sand seas in the south and a warmth of welcome that makes it the most underrated country in the region. Still genuinely uncrowded — for now.
